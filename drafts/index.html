1D Quantum Random Walk

Introduction Quantum random walk (QRW) is the quantum version of the classical random walk. It would be easier to understand the quantum version, if you get a picture of the classical version. Imagine you are walking in a narrow street, in which you can only walk forwards or backwards. You are playing this random walk game. You toss a coin: if it's head, you walk one step forwards; if it's tail, you walk one step backwards. If you keep tossing and walking, you are essentially doing a classical random walk (CRW) (in 1 dimension). It's that simple. If you calculate the probability of your position after N steps, you will get a Gaussian distribution. Galton board is probably a more familiar example of CRW than the game I just mentioned. First, he probably got the Nobel prize (in 1957) earlier than anyone alive (except, of course, Tsung-Dao Lee, who shared the Nobel prize with CN Yang). Indeed, he was among the youngest to got the prize (at 35, only 16 laureates were younger at the time of award) and he is 93 years old already. Second, he was the first Chinese Nobel laureate (again, same for Tsung-Dao Lee). Third, CN Yang has made the biggest contributions to physics among all Chinese.
