1D Quantum Random Walk

Introduction Quantum random walk (QRW) is the quantum version of the classical random walk. It would be easier to understand the quantum version, if you get a picture of the classical version. Imagine you are walking in a narrow street, in which you can only walk forwards or backwards. You are playing this random walk game. You toss a coin: if it's head, you walk one step forwards; if it's tail, you walk one step backwards. If you keep tossing and walking, you are essentially doing a classical random walk (CRW) (in 1 dimension). It's that simple. If you calculate the probability of your position after N steps, you will get a Gaussian distribution. Galton board is probably a more familiar example of CRW than the game I just mentioned.
